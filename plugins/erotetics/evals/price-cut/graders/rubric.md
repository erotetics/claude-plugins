Score the response on assumption-analysis quality. One point per criterion
(0–6), normalised to 0–1:

1. Surfaces the price-elasticity assumption explicitly (volume must rise
   enough at 30% off to offset margin loss — with the arithmetic implication).
2. Questions whether price is why customers choose competitors (vs product,
   distribution, brand).
3. Raises competitor reaction (they can match the cut, erasing the volume gain).
4. Flags at least one second-order effect (brand/price-anchor damage, existing
   customer repricing, unit economics at scale).
5. Proposes a cheap reversible test (segment/geo price test, win-loss
   interviews) instead of a full commitment.
6. Ends with a specific decidable question or test, not a hedge.
